## KEY COPYWRITING TECHNIQUES (Across All Brands)

### 1. THE "SO WHAT?" TEST
Every sentence answers "so what?" for the reader. Apple doesn't say "M5 chip"â€”they say "Supercharged." Benefits over features.

### 2. SPECIFICITY SELLLS
"97 min" beats "save time." "$1.4T" beats "lots of transactions." Numbers create credibility.

### 3. SECOND-PERSON DOMINANCE
The best copy uses "you" extensively. It makes it personal. Slack: "your schedule," "your work."

### 4. ACTIVE VERBS OVER PASSIVE
"Supercharged by M5" beats "Powered by M5." Nike: "Make a name." Trello: "unleash your productivity."

### 5. CONTRAST CREATES MEANING
"From prep to post-shave" (DSC)
"Direct and wholesale" (Shopify)
"Online and in person" (Shopify)

### 6. THE 3-WORD HEADLINE RULE
Most winning headlines: 3-5 words
- "Just Do It"
- "Belong anywhere"
- "Find anything."

### 7. EMOTIONAL CATEGORIZATION
Headspace: "Stress less / Sleep soundly / Manage anxiety"
One word. Real emotions. Specific needs.

### 8. SOCIAL PROOF AS STORY
Stripe doesn't just say "big"â€”they show Hertz, Instacart, Substack.

### 9. THE BENEFIT STACK
Stack multiple benefits in one sentence:
"Sharper Blades. Smoother Shaves. Better Prices."

### 10. REDUCE FEAR BEFORE ASK
"Pause or cancel anytime" (DSC)
"No developer, no problem" (Unbounce)
"Cancel anytime" (Unbounce)

## ACTIONABLE SKILLS LEO SHOULD LEARN

### SKILL 1: The 5-Minute Headline Generator
Write 20 headlines in 5 minutes. Don't edit. Quantity first. Then pick the best 3.

**Practice:** Every morning, write 10 headlines for your current project before checking email.

### SKILL 2: Benefit Translation
Take any feature and write 3 benefit versions:
- Feature: "AI-powered search"
- Benefit: "Find anything in seconds"
- Emotional: "Never lose a file again"

**Practice:** Create a "feature â†’ benefit â†’ emotion" worksheet for every product you write about.

### SKILL 3: The "So What?" Test
After writing any paragraph, ask "So what?" until you reach the core benefit.

**Practice:** Edit existing copy by cutting every sentence that fails the "So What?" test.

### SKILL 4: Voice Calibration
Read your copy out loud. Does it sound like a human talking to a friend, or a corporation shouting?

**Practice:** Record yourself reading copy. Listen back. Does it feel natural?

### SKILL 5: Specificity Practice
Replace vague words with specific numbers:
- "Many customers" â†’ "4,000+ leading organizations"
- "Fast" â†’ "in seconds"
- "Save time" â†’ "save 97 minutes per week"

**Practice:** Audit your copy for vague terms. Replace each with a specific alternative.

### SKILL 6: The Headline Handoff
Write a headline that makes the subhead feel inevitable:
- Headline: "Find anything. Protect everything."
- Subhead: "Find, organize, and protect your work with Dropbox Dash."

The headline creates a question the subhead answers.

**Practice:** Write headlines first, then let subheads explain them.

### SKILL 7: Friction Identification
List every reason someone might NOT buy/click/sign up. Address each one in your copy.

**Practice:** Make a "Objection List" for every project. Answer each in your copy.

### SKILL 8: The First-Word Test
Count how many sentences start with "We" or "Our." Flip them to "You" or "Your."

**Practice:** Edit all "We do X" to "You get X."

### SKILL 9: Rhythm Variation
Read copy with emphasis on different words. The meaning shouldn't changeâ€”but the feeling should.

**Practice:** Read your copy to a friend. Ask where they got bored.

### SKILL 10: The Competitor Flip
Write your copy as if you're the customer who tried the competitor and was disappointed.

**Practice:** Switch POV. Write from user's frustation with alternatives.
